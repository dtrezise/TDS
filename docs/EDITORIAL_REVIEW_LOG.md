# Editorial review log

This internal log preserves claims that were corrected, narrowed, excluded, or held for more evidence before publication. It is not a public-facing corrections section and it does not manufacture an approval history. Each entry records the risk, the defensible treatment, and whether follow-up remains open.

## Operating rule

When a proposed claim outruns the record, editors should take one of three actions:

1. Narrow it to the strongest sourceable statement and preserve material limiting context.
2. Exclude it from publication and record the reason here.
3. Hold it for additional research with a specific evidence requirement.

Public pages should explain standards and uncertainty where that helps readers interpret the evidence. They should not display a running list of rejected slogans merely to dramatize editorial restraint.
